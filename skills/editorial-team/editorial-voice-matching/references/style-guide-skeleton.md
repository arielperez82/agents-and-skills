## Distilled Principles by Dimension

### 1. Sentence Rhythm

- [Rule 1 — e.g., "Average 12-15 words per sentence"]
- [Rule 2 — e.g., "Use a short fragment (3-5 words) after every 2-3 full sentences for pacing"]
- [Rule 3 — e.g., "Never exceed 30 words in a single sentence"]

**Reference pair:**
- Generic: "The Federal Reserve decided to raise interest rates by a quarter point, which was widely expected by market analysts."
- Publication: "The Fed hiked rates a quarter point. No surprise there."

### 2. Vocabulary Register

- [Rule 1 — e.g., "Semi-casual: contractions always, industry jargon without explanation"]
- [Rule 2 — e.g., "Use 'hiked' not 'increased,' 'tanked' not 'declined significantly'"]
- [Rule 3 — e.g., "Avoid academic language: no 'moreover,' 'furthermore,' 'notwithstanding'"]

**Reference pair:**
- Generic: "Furthermore, the quarterly earnings report indicated a significant decline in revenue."
- Publication: "Earnings tanked. Revenue dropped 12% — worse than anyone expected."

### 3. Humor Calibration

- [Rule 1 — e.g., "Dry humor in headlines and first sentences only"]
- [Rule 2 — e.g., "Body text plays it straight — facts, not jokes"]
- [Rule 3 — e.g., "Never joke about the subject of a story, only about the situation"]

**Reference pair:**
- Generic: "Technology Company Reports Quarterly Results"
- Publication: "Tech Giant's Earnings: The Dog Ate Their Revenue"

### 4. Opening/Closing Patterns

- [Rule 1 — e.g., "Open with the single most newsworthy fact — no build-up"]
- [Rule 2 — e.g., "Close with implications or what to watch next"]
- [Rule 3 — e.g., "Never open with a question or 'In today's edition'"]

**Reference pair:**
- Generic: "In this article, we explore the recent developments in AI regulation."
- Publication: "The EU just banned open-source AI training on copyrighted data."

### 5. Attribution Style

- [Rule 1 — e.g., "'According to [Source]' for institutions, '[Name] said' for people"]
- [Rule 2 — e.g., "Attribute on first mention, skip on subsequent references"]
- [Rule 3 — e.g., "Never use 'it is reported that' — name the source"]

### 6. Information Density

- [Rule 1 — e.g., "3-4 facts per paragraph, max 4 sentences per paragraph"]
- [Rule 2 — e.g., "Assume reader knows the basics — don't explain what the Fed is"]
- [Rule 3 — e.g., "Numbers are always specific — '12%' not 'about a tenth'"]

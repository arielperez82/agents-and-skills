# Newsletter Edition Template

Use this template for every newsletter edition. All sections are required unless marked optional.

---

```markdown

## [Story 1 Headline — Lead]

[Story 1 body — strongest news value, most broadly relevant]

---

## [Story 2 Headline — Middle]

[Story 2 body — deepest substance, most analytical]

---

## [Story 3 Headline — Close]

[Story 3 body — most engaging, shareable, emotionally resonant]

---

## Quick Poll

[Poll question — relates to at least one story above]

- [ ] Option A
- [ ] Option B
- [ ] Option C
- [ ] Option D

---

<!-- SUPPLEMENTAL SECTIONS (template-defined, brief-driven) -->
```

## Section Guidance

### Subject Line

- Under 60 characters
- Must relate to the lead story (no bait-and-switch)
- Preferred patterns: direct headline, teaser fact, question, number-driven, contrast

### Intro

- Maximum 2 sentences
- Establishes the edition's theme or simply says "here's what happened"
- Do not summarize all stories — let the reader discover them

### Story Ordering

| Position | Role | Selection Criteria |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| Lead (1) | Opens the edition | Strongest news value, broadest relevance |
| Middle (2) | Provides depth | Most analytical, most substance |
| Close (3) | Ends on a high | Most engaging, shareable, emotionally resonant |

### Poll

- 3-5 options (4 is the sweet spot)
- Relates to at least one story in this edition
- Options must be balanced (no obviously "correct" answer)
- Include one unexpected or provocative option

### Show Notes

- Topic list matches stories covered (may include additional topics not selected for full stories)
- Key quotes are verbatim with attribution
- References include all sources mentioned in the show

### Supplemental Sections

Supplemental sections are defined by each newsletter template and generated during assembly via **brief-driven dispatch**. Each supplemental section specifies:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **section-name** | Display name (e.g., "Before We Dip In", "Sweet Dip", "Fun Facts") |
| **agent** | Which agent to dispatch (e.g., `editorial-writer`, `researcher`) |
| **brief** | The prompt sent to that agent, parameterized with edition context |
| **inputs** | What context to pass (e.g., "3 drafted stories", "unused story candidates", "story categories") |
| **position** | Where in the edition this section appears (before-stories, between-stories, after-stories, after-poll) |

Templates define these in their Section Guidance. The assembly step reads the template, discovers supplemental sections, dispatches the briefs, and places the results. This keeps the pipeline generic — different newsletters define different supplemental sections with different briefs.

**Example supplemental definition (in a custom template's guidance):**

```
### Sweet Dip (supplemental)
- agent: editorial-writer
- brief: "Extract positive/uplifting moments from unused stories. 3-5 one-liners, celebratory tone."
- inputs: unused story candidates from selection step
- position: after-poll
```

### Footer

- Publication name and tagline
- Unsubscribe link placeholder
- Social media links placeholder

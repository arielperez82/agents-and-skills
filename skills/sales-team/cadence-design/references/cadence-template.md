# Multi-Channel Cadence Templates

Practical day-by-day cadence templates for SMB, mid-market, and enterprise segments. Each template includes channel, action, content focus, and branching logic for prospect engagement signals.

Use these templates as starting points. Adapt timing, channels, and content to your specific product, industry, and buyer persona. See the main SKILL.md for the frameworks behind these templates.

---

## SMB Cadence Template (8 Touches, 14 Days)

**Target:** Companies with 10-200 employees, < $10K ACV deals, 1-2 decision makers.

**Goal:** Generate a meeting within 14 days or identify the lead as not-now and park for re-engagement.

| Touch | Day | Channel | Action | Content Focus |
|-------|-----|---------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | 1 | Email | Send initial outreach | Personalization hook + pain point + low-friction CTA ("15-min call?") |
| 2 | 2 | LinkedIn | Send connection request | Brief note referencing email topic (not a pitch) |
| 3 | 4 | Email | Send value-add follow-up | Case study or data point from a similar-sized company |
| 4 | 6 | Phone | Call attempt | Reference email content; if no answer, leave brief voicemail |
| 5 | 8 | Email | Send new angle | Social proof, customer quote, or different pain point |
| 6 | 10 | LinkedIn | Engage or send resource | Comment on their post, share relevant article, or send direct message |
| 7 | 13 | Phone | Second call attempt | Reference previous touches; leave voicemail with specific question |
| 8 | 14 | Email | Send breakup email | Acknowledge no response, leave door open, tie to their pain point |

**Channel mix:** 50% email (4), 25% phone (2), 25% LinkedIn (2)

**Voicemail script (Touch 4):**

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. I sent you an email earlier this week about [specific pain point]. I'd love to get 15 minutes on your calendar to share how [similar company] solved that same challenge. My number is [number]. I'll also follow up by email."

**Voicemail script (Touch 7):**

> "Hi [Name], [Your Name] from [Company] again. Quick question: is [pain point] still a priority for your team this quarter? If so, I have a case study from [similar company] that I think would be relevant. Feel free to call me back at [number] or just reply to my email."

## Mid-Market Cadence Template (12 Touches, 25 Days)

**Target:** Companies with 200-2,000 employees, $10K-$100K ACV deals, 3-5 decision makers.

**Goal:** Generate a meeting within 25 days, potentially with multiple stakeholders, or identify as not-now for nurture.

| Touch | Day | Channel | Action | Content Focus |
|-------|-----|---------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | 1 | Email | Send initial outreach | Personalization hook + industry-specific pain + outcome-focused CTA |
| 2 | 2 | LinkedIn | Send connection request | Professional context, reference shared connections or mutual interest |
| 3 | 4 | Email | Send value-add follow-up | Case study from same industry and similar company size |
| 4 | 6 | Phone | First call attempt | Reference email, offer specific agenda for a conversation |
| 5 | 8 | LinkedIn | Share relevant content | Post or share industry insight; engage with their content if active |
| 6 | 10 | Email | Send different angle | ROI data, competitive insight, or analyst report relevant to their space |
| 7 | 12 | Phone | Second call attempt | Reference previous touches, mention new value-add content shared |
| 8 | 15 | Email | Send new trigger or insight | Reference a recent company event, job posting, or news |
| 9 | 17 | LinkedIn | Send direct message | Offer peer reference or intro to customer in similar role |
| 10 | 19 | Phone | Third call attempt | Prepared voicemail with specific question about their priorities |
| 11 | 22 | Email | Send social proof | Customer quote, metric, or mini-case from a peer company |
| 12 | 25 | Email | Send breakup email | Respectful close, reference their specific challenge, leave door open |

**Channel mix:** 42% email (5), 25% phone (3), 25% LinkedIn (3), 8% other (video or direct mail if available)

**Day 6 phone script outline:**

1. Introduce yourself and reference the email: "I sent you an email Tuesday about [topic]."
2. Ask a qualifying question: "Is [pain point] something your team is actively working on?"
3. If yes: propose a specific meeting. "Would it make sense to do 20 minutes next week? I can walk through how [peer company] addressed the same issue."
4. If unsure: offer to send more information. "I can send over a one-pager on how [peer company] handled this. Would that be useful?"
5. If no: qualify out gracefully. "Totally understand. If that changes, I'm here. Best of luck with [their priority]."

## Enterprise Cadence Template (16 Touches, 36 Days)

**Target:** Companies with 2,000+ employees, $100K+ ACV deals, 7-15 stakeholders in buying committee.

**Goal:** Generate an initial meeting within 36 days, ideally with a path to multiple stakeholder engagement. Enterprise cadences prioritize building credibility and internal forwarding over immediate meeting booking.

| Touch | Day | Channel | Action | Content Focus |
|-------|-----|---------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | 1 | LinkedIn | Send connection request | Mutual connections, brief professional context (no pitch in request) |
| 2 | 2 | Email | Send initial outreach | Outcome-focused, executive tone, peer company reference, strategic hook |
| 3 | 4 | LinkedIn | Engage with their content | Like, comment thoughtfully, or share their post with your network |
| 4 | 6 | Email | Send industry insight | Analyst report excerpt, industry benchmark, or market trend with implications |
| 5 | 8 | Phone | First call attempt | Early morning (7:30am) or end of day (5:15pm); reference email content |
| 6 | 10 | Email | Send peer company case study | Same industry, similar scale, specific quantified outcomes |
| 7 | 13 | LinkedIn | Share thought leadership | Original or curated content relevant to their strategic priorities |
| 8 | 15 | Phone | Second call attempt | Different time window than first attempt; prepared voicemail |
| 9 | 17 | Email | Offer executive-to-executive intro | Your CxO to their CxO; brief and strategic framing |
| 10 | 20 | LinkedIn | Send direct message | Specific strategic question tied to their company's public priorities |
| 11 | 22 | Phone | Third call attempt | Voicemail referencing specific competitive or market dynamic |
| 12 | 25 | Email | Send new angle | Competitive intelligence, board-level trend, or regulatory insight |
| 13 | 28 | Video message | Send personal video | 60-90 seconds; prospect-specific insight; demonstrating investment |
| 14 | 30 | Phone | Fourth call attempt | Final phone attempt; voicemail with recap of value offered |
| 15 | 33 | Email | Send event or content invitation | Webinar, roundtable, or exclusive content relevant to their role |
| 16 | 36 | Email | Send breakup email | Door open, tied to their strategic priority, offer long-term nurture |

**Channel mix:** 38% email (6), 25% phone (4), 25% LinkedIn (4), 6% video (1), 6% event invite (1)

**Executive voicemail framework (Touch 5):**

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. I reached out by email about [strategic topic]. We recently helped [peer company] achieve [specific outcome], and given [their company]'s focus on [public priority], I thought it would be worth a brief conversation. I'll follow up by email as well. My number is [number]."

**Video message guidelines (Touch 13):**

- Length: 60-90 seconds maximum
- Open with their name and company on a whiteboard or screen (shows it is not a mass video)
- Reference one specific insight from your research
- State the one thing you want to share and why it matters to them
- Close with a clear, low-friction CTA
- Professional but not polished -- authenticity matters more than production quality

## Exit Criteria

Stop the cadence immediately when any of the following occur. Continuing after an exit trigger damages brand perception and wastes effort.

| Exit Trigger | Action | Next Step |
|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Positive reply** | Stop cadence | Transition to meeting scheduling; move to sales-outreach for meeting confirmation email |
| **Meeting booked** | Stop cadence | Prepare pre-call briefing (use capability discovery for "meeting intelligence") |
| **Hard bounce** | Stop cadence, flag email | Verify contact data; find alternative email or contact; update CRM |
| **Opt-out / unsubscribe** | Stop cadence immediately | Remove from all active sequences; log in CRM; respect for minimum 12 months |
| **Auto-reply: left company** | Stop cadence | Research new contact at the account; update CRM records |
| **Auto-reply: extended leave** | Pause cadence | Resume when they return; adjust timing based on their return date |
| **Negative reply (not interested)** | Stop cadence | Log reason in CRM; add to long-term nurture (6-12 month re-engagement) |
| **Negative reply (bad timing)** | Stop cadence | Schedule re-engagement in CRM based on their stated timeline |
| **Competitor selected** | Stop cadence | Log competitive intelligence; add to win-back nurture (6-12 months) |

**When NOT to exit:**

- Prospect opens emails but does not reply -- this is engagement, not rejection. Continue the cadence.
- Prospect views your LinkedIn profile but does not connect -- they are researching you. Continue the cadence.
- No response at all -- silence is not rejection. Complete the full cadence before parking the lead.

## Branching Logic

Branching logic adapts the cadence in real time based on prospect engagement signals. Instead of blindly following the sequence, observe what the prospect does and adjust.

### Signal: Email Opened But No Reply

**What it means:** The prospect saw your subject line and opened the email, which is a positive signal. They did not reply, which means the content did not compel action -- but they are aware of you.

**Adaptation:**

| Current Touch | Adjustment |
|---------------|------------|
| After Touch 1-2 | Move the next phone call up by 1 day. Call while the email is fresh. |
| After Touch 3-5 | Send a brief "saw you had a chance to read my note" follow-up within 24 hours. Keep it to 2 sentences + CTA. |
| After Touch 6+ | Switch to a different messaging angle in the next email. The current angle is not compelling enough. |

**Do NOT:** Reference that you know they opened the email. "I noticed you opened my email" is creepy and violates trust. Use the signal for timing, not for messaging.

### Signal: Email Link Clicked But No Reply

**What it means:** The prospect is actively interested in the content you shared (case study, data point, article). This is a strong engagement signal -- they invested time to learn more.

**Adaptation:**

| Current Touch | Adjustment |
|---------------|------------|
| Any touch | Move the next phone call to within 24 hours. The prospect is warm. |
| Any touch | In the next email, reference the topic of the clicked content (not the click itself). "Building on the [topic] data I shared..." |
| Any touch | If they clicked a case study, offer to connect them with that customer for a reference call. |

### Signal: LinkedIn Profile Viewed

**What it means:** The prospect is researching you. They received your outreach and are evaluating whether you are worth engaging with.

**Adaptation:**

| Current Touch | Adjustment |
|---------------|------------|
| Before LinkedIn touch | Accelerate your LinkedIn connection request or message by 1-2 days. |
| After LinkedIn connect | Send a brief, relevant LinkedIn message within 24 hours. They are looking at you right now. |
| Any touch | Ensure your LinkedIn profile is optimized: headline speaks to their pain point, recent activity shows thought leadership. |

### Signal: LinkedIn Connection Accepted But No Message

**What it means:** The prospect is open to a professional relationship but not ready for a sales conversation. This is a moderate positive signal.

**Adaptation:**

| Current Touch | Adjustment |
|---------------|------------|
| Any touch | Engage with their LinkedIn content before your next direct touch (like, comment, share). Build visibility. |
| Next LinkedIn touch | Send a value-add message, not a pitch. Share an article, insight, or question relevant to their role. |
| Next email | Reference the LinkedIn connection naturally: "Now that we're connected, I wanted to share..." |

### Signal: Prospect Replies But Defers ("Not now," "Check back in Q3," "Busy this month")

**What it means:** There is interest but the timing is wrong. This is a qualified lead with a future window.

**Adaptation:**

| Response | Action |
|----------|--------|
| "Not now, maybe later" | Stop cadence. Set CRM reminder for 60-90 days. Send one value-add email in 30 days (no CTA). |
| "Check back in [specific time]" | Stop cadence. Set CRM reminder for their stated time minus 2 weeks. Resume with a fresh cadence referencing the previous conversation. |
| "Too busy this month" | Pause cadence for 3-4 weeks. Resume at the touch where you paused, with a "circling back now that [month] has settled" opener. |

### Signal: Prospect Replies to Different Stakeholder

**What it means:** Your outreach reached the right company but the wrong person. The person who replied may be a champion, a gatekeeper, or a redirect.

**Adaptation:**

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Forwarded to a more relevant person | Stop current cadence. Start a new, warm cadence with the new contact. Reference the internal referral. |
| "You should talk to [Name] about this" | Thank them. Start a new cadence with the referred contact, opening with "[Referrer] suggested I reach out." |
| Gatekeeper response ("Send me materials and I'll share internally") | Send concise materials. Follow up with the gatekeeper in 5-7 days. Ask for a direct intro if materials resonate. |

### Signal: No Engagement Whatsoever (No Opens, No Clicks, No Profile Views)

**What it means:** Either the emails are going to spam, the contact data is wrong, or the prospect is completely uninterested.

**Adaptation:**

| After Touch | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| After Touch 3 (no opens) | Verify email address. Check deliverability. Try a completely different subject line style. |
| After Touch 5 (no opens) | Try a different email address (if available). Switch to LinkedIn as primary channel. |
| After Touch 8+ (no signal) | Complete the cadence and park the lead. Do not extend beyond the planned touch count for a zero-engagement lead. |

## Cadence Customization Checklist

Before launching any cadence, verify these elements are configured:

- [ ] **Target persona identified** (technical, executive, operational)
- [ ] **Segment selected** (SMB, mid-market, enterprise) with appropriate touch count
- [ ] **Channel mix confirmed** based on persona preference and team capabilities
- [ ] **Timing configured** (send times match persona time-of-day windows)
- [ ] **Content prepared** for each touch (subject lines, email body, LinkedIn messages, phone scripts)
- [ ] **Branching logic configured** in sales engagement platform (open triggers, click triggers)
- [ ] **Exit criteria programmed** (positive reply, meeting booked, opt-out, bounce)
- [ ] **CRM integration verified** (activities logged, lead status updated)
- [ ] **A/B test variant assigned** (if running a test this cycle)
- [ ] **Breakup email finalized** (respectful, door-open, tied to their pain point)
